# External Critique Summary: Landscape Document

Source: Perplexity review (captured April 2026)
Status: Condensed audit note

## Overall Assessment

Strong vision document, weak scholarly review in its earlier form.

## What Was Strong

- Clear practical thesis: local, semantically grounded, failure-aware neuro-symbolic systems.
- Concrete case-study framing made architecture and workflows understandable.
- Reproducibility and transparency were treated as core concerns.

## Main Issues Identified

- Overstated claims without enough citations or methods.
- Repeated broad statements that read as consensus without evidence.
- Mixed genres in one document: survey, tutorial, manifesto, and recruiting pitch.
- Technical metrics presented without sufficient evaluation context.
- Tone drift from scholarly to promotional in multiple sections.

## Recommended Corrections

1. Split the content into two artifacts:
   - Neutral landscape review.
   - Separate implementation/pitch document.
2. Attach citations or soften unsupported claims.
3. Distinguish facts, internal observations, and hypotheses.
4. Add limitations and methods context for metrics.
5. Remove repeated thesis statements and compress structure.

## Actions Completed in This Repository

- Rewrote the landscape review to an evidence-aware, concise format.
- Moved implementation advocacy into a separate pitch document.
- Reduced rhetorical overreach and added explicit caution language.

## Archive Intent

This note is kept as an audit trail to preserve rationale for the rewrite and prevent regression in future edits.
